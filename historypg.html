History of San Diego's Natural Resources

Discover how San Diego has worked to protect its natural resources over time, from air and water quality to land preservation.

Water Conservation

Water conservation is a critical concern in San Diego, where the majority of water resources are imported. Over the years, the San Diego County Water Authority has led efforts to conserve water through resource conservation and management programs. Learn more about the SDCWA and the history of water challenges in San Diego, beginning from the 18th century.

Air Quality

Air quality in San Diego is a pressing environmental issue affected by pollution from vehicle emissions, industrial activities, and wildfires. Pollutants such as particulate matter, ozone, and nitrogen oxides can have severe impacts on public health, contribute to respiratory problems, heart disease, and exacerbate conditions like asthma. Learn more about San Diego's current and historic air quality data.

Learn More

Marine Conservation

Marine conservation in San Diego plays a crucial role in preserving the region’s marine biodiversity. The Matlahuayl State Marine Reserve (SMR) and San Diego-Scripps Coastal State Marine Conservation Area (SMCA), both located by UCSD, are two designated Marine Protected Areas which help protect diverse marine communities. Learn about the natural and cultural history of these two areas.

Wildfires

Climate change will likely increase the frequency and severity of wildfires in San Diego, due to drought and rising temperatures. These fires pose a major threat to ecosystems, homes, and communities, causing significant environmental, financial, social, and health impacts. Learn more about the history of major fires that have occurred in San Diego.
